# Why We Throttle Our Own AI

Most autonomous systems are pushed to be faster.  
Lower latency.  
Snappier reactions.  
Better-looking demos.

That works in software.

It breaks down in physical systems.

We had to choose between minimizing latency and minimizing variance.  
Speed versus consistency.

For Nomi, consistency wins.

Erratic speed feels unsafe.  
Stable delay feels intentional.

A companion that reacts instantly most of the time  
but hesitates occasionally  
feels broken.

A companion that reacts slightly slower  
but always the same  
feels reliable.

So we deliberately deprioritize raw execution speed  
in favor of deterministic behavior.

Users adapt to rhythm.  
They don’t adapt to randomness.

In deep-tech hardware, trust doesn’t come from being fast.  
It comes from being predictable.

At Osocius Dynamics, we don’t optimize for impressive numbers.  
We optimize for knowing what happens next.
